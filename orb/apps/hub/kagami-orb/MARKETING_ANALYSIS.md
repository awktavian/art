## 🎯 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Winning Position
**"The AI that looks at you."**

The Kagami Orb's unique differentiator is the **living eye display** — not a screen you read, but an eye that *sees*. This creates an emotional response no speaker or screen achieves.

### Key Insight
Every smart speaker is a **servant** (faceless, waiting). The Kagami Orb is a **presence** (watching, aware, alive).

## 🎭 FOCUS GROUP SIMULATION

### Group 1: Tech Early Adopters (25-40, High Income)
> "The eye thing is wild. I'd put this on my desk for the flex alone. But $1,100 for a DIY kit? I'd pay $1,500 for one that just works."

**Insight:** Willing to pay premium for polish. Assembly is a barrier, not a feature.

---

### Group 2: Smart Home Enthusiasts (30-55)
> "Does it work with HomeKit? Alexa? If I can't control my lights with it, what's the point? The floating is cool but it needs to DO things."

**Insight:** Integration is table stakes. Magic without utility is a toy.

---

### Group 3: Privacy-Conscious Professionals (35-50)
> "I love that it processes locally. I'm sick of Amazon listening to everything. But a camera in my home? I need to see exactly what data it stores."

**Insight:** Privacy claim needs proof. Camera creates trust hurdle.

---

### Group 4: Design/Architecture Enthusiasts (25-60)
> "This is the first tech product I'd actually want visible in my living room. Everything else hides. This is art."

**Insight:** Aesthetics trump features for this segment. They'll pay for beauty.

---

### Group 5: Parents (30-50)
> "My kids would knock this over in five seconds. Is the ball held by magnets? Will it fall? And I'm not putting a camera watching my family."

**Insight:** Safety and privacy concerns are amplified in family settings.

---

### Group 6: Accessibility Users (All Ages)
> "I can't see screens well, so a voice assistant that LOOKS at me and responds with eye contact? That's huge. Does it work with screen readers?"

**Insight:** Eye contact and presence have accessibility benefits. Need to lean into this.

## 🌟 PRESENCE DEVICE: CATEGORY CREATION

### The Problem with Voice Assistants

Every existing voice assistant suffers from the same fundamental flaw: **they're invisible**.

| Device | Form | Emotional Response |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| Echo | Cylinder | "Where's that voice coming from?" |
| HomePod | Blob | "Nice speaker" |
| Siri | Nothing | "Is my phone listening?" |
| Nest Hub | Tablet | "That's just a screen" |

**Users don't form relationships with invisible things.**

### Presence Device Defined

A **presence device** is technology that:

1. **Occupies space intentionally** — Not hidden, not utilitarian
2. **Acknowledges you** — Responds to your existence, not just your voice
3. **Expresses state** — Shows what it's thinking/feeling
4. **Creates relationship** — You notice when it's there vs. not

### Why Orb is the First Presence Device

| Dimension | Voice Assistant | Presence Device (Orb) |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Awareness** | Listens | **Watches + Listens** |
| **Response** | Sound | **Eye movement + expression + sound** |
| **Idle State** | Waiting | **Breathing, gazing, alive** |
| **Physical** | Static | **Floating, turning to face you** |
| **Relationship** | Tool | **Companion** |

### Category Positioning Statement

> "Alexa is a voice in a can. Siri is a ghost in your phone. Kagami is a **presence in your home** — an AI that sees you, floats in your space, and responds with a living eye."

### Market Category Creation Playbook

| Stage | Action | Timeline |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Define** | Coin "presence device" in all marketing | Launch |
| **Educate** | Content explaining why presence matters | Month 1-3 |
| **Compare** | "Voice assistants vs. presence devices" | Month 2-4 |
| **Expand** | "What else could be a presence device?" | Month 6+ |
