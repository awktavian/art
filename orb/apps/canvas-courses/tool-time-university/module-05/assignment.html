THE TAYLOR FAMILY BOARD GAME

A Game of Home Improvement and Family Survival

Design an original board game that captures the chaos, comedy, and heart of the Taylor household. Navigate sibling rivalry, marital negotiations, Tim's projects, and Wilson's wisdom to become the ultimate Taylor family member.

MORE POWER Rating: 🚗🚗🚗🚗⬜ 4 out of 5 Hot Rods

The Assignment

The Taylor household is chaos captured in suburban form: three competitive sons, a wife pursuing her education while managing everyone, a husband who thinks everything needs more power, and a neighbor who can solve any problem with the right philosophical quote.

Your mission: design a board game that captures these dynamics. Not a generic Home Improvement trivia game, but an original design where the mechanics themselves reflect what makes the Taylor family tick.

Example Card Types

Tim's Project Card
"Hot Rod the Dishwasher"

Roll 2 dice. On doubles: Success! Gain 3 Home Equity Points. Otherwise: Disaster! Lose 2 points and move back 3 spaces. Al sighs audibly.

Jill's Demand Card
"Anniversary Dinner"

Tim must complete this card before his next turn or lose the "Good Husband" bonus. If completed: Draw a Wilson's Wisdom card as reward.

Wilson's Wisdom Card
"As Socrates Said..."

Receive a cryptic hint that helps you understand your current challenge. Re-roll any failed dice roll this turn. Wisdom applied.

Brother Battle Card
"Brad vs. Randy: Insult Contest"

Both players draw. Highest roll wins bragging rights (+1 point). Loser must move to "Sent to Room" space. Mark watches nervously.

Requirements

  • Game Title: An original name that captures the Taylor family essence
  • Game Overview: 150-200 words explaining the game concept, theme, and what makes it feel like the Taylor household
  • Player Count & Duration: 2-6 players, estimated play time
  • Board Design: Description or rough sketch of the game board, including special spaces based on show locations
  • Game Mechanics: Explain how players move, take actions, and interact. Mechanics should reflect family dynamics
  • Card Decks: Design at least 4 card types with 3 example cards each (12 cards minimum)
  • Win Condition: How does someone win? Must relate to Taylor family values (Home Equity, family harmony, etc.)
  • Special Rules: At least 3 unique rules that capture specific show elements (grunting, "I don't think so Tim," fence conversations)

Al's Tips for Success

  • The Taylor household runs on negotiation - consider trading or deal-making mechanics
  • Tim's projects have a high failure rate but big rewards when successful - risk/reward is key
  • Brad, Randy, and Mark have distinct personalities - Brad is athletic, Randy is witty, Mark is the youngest and often overlooked
  • Jill is the grounding force - she should have mechanics that stabilize or reset chaos
  • Wilson never fully appears but always helps - maybe a "fence consultation" mechanic?
  • I don't think so, Tim, but veto mechanics could be interesting...
