What was the original working title for "Home Improvement" before legal issues forced a name change?
Correct! ARUH ARUH ARUH!

The show was originally named "Hammer Time" after MC Hammer's catchphrase, but legal concerns led to the change to "Home Improvement."

Not quite, Tim...

The correct answer is Hammer Time. The show was named after MC Hammer's catchphrase, but legal issues forced the name change.

Question 2 of 10 (7.5 HED)
Multiple Choice
Which actress was originally cast as Jill Taylor in the unaired pilot before Patricia Richardson took over the role?
Correct! Al would be proud!

Frances Fisher filmed the original unaired pilot. After test audiences responded poorly to the chemistry, Patricia Richardson was cast instead. Fisher later appeared in Titanic and Unforgiven.

I don't think so, Tim...

The correct answer is Frances Fisher. She filmed the original pilot, but Patricia Richardson was recast after test screenings.

Question 3 of 10 (7.5 HED)
True or False
Richard Karn's character Al Borland was originally intended to be only a one-episode guest appearance, not a permanent cast member.
Correct! Tool Time wisdom!

Richard Karn's chemistry with Tim Allen was so good during his one-episode guest spot that producers made Al Borland a permanent fixture on the show.

That's a safety violation!

This is True. Al was only supposed to appear once, but the chemistry between Karn and Allen made him essential to the show.

The Taylor family's mysterious neighbor who dispenses wisdom over the fence is named _______ W. Wilson Jr.

Hint: His first name matches his last name!

Hidey-ho, neighbor!

Wilson W. Wilson Jr., played by Earl Hindman, became television's most beloved fence-obscured philosopher. His full face was never shown during the series (until the final curtain call).

Question 8 of 10 (7.5 HED)
True or False
Wilson's full face is frequently shown throughout Season 1, with the fence gimmick not starting until Season 2.
Correct! You've been watching carefully!

Wilson's face was always partially obscured by the fence from the very beginning of the series. Earl Hindman's full face was only revealed during the final curtain call in the series finale.

Look closer at that fence!

This is False. Wilson's face was hidden from the pilot episode onward - it became one of the show's signature visual gags.

Binford Tools, the fictional sponsor of Tool Time, is based in which city - a location chosen for its connection to manufacturing heritage?
Motor City Pride! Correct!
