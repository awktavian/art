Answer Key (For Instructor Reference)

Note: In Canvas, this section would be hidden from students and visible only after submission.

1. B Hammer Time - The show was originally named after MC Hammer's catchphrase, but legal concerns led to the change to "Home Improvement."
2. B Frances Fisher - Fisher filmed the original unaired pilot. After test audiences responded poorly to the chemistry, Patricia Richardson was cast instead.
3. B A one-episode guest appearance - Richard Karn's chemistry with Tim Allen was so good that producers made Al a permanent fixture.
4. B Stand-up comedy - Tim Allen was a successful stand-up comedian whose "Men Are Pigs" routine caught Disney's attention.
5. B September 17, 1991 - The pilot premiered on ABC on this date, drawing 16.9 million viewers.
6. B Brad, Randy, Mark - Brad (Zachery Ty Bryan) is the oldest, Randy (Jonathan Taylor Thomas) is the middle child, and Mark (Taran Noah Smith) is the youngest.
7. B Detroit, Michigan - Tool Time is a local Detroit cable show, and Binford Tools is headquartered there, fitting the city's manufacturing heritage.
8. B Wilson W. Wilson Jr.; his face is always partially obscured by the fence - Earl Hindman played Wilson, whose full face was never shown on the series (until the final curtain call).
9. B "More power!" - Tim's signature catchphrase, often accompanied by his trademark grunt, encapsulates his philosophy of supercharging everything.
10. C The dishwasher - Tim's unauthorized modifications to increase water pressure and add a "turbo cycle" result in a spectacular failure, setting the tone for the series.
