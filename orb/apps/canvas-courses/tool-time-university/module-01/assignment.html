THE BINFORD SAFETY REPORT

Dangerous Tool Design & Disaster Prevention Analysis

Your mission: Design a Binford tool that Tim Taylor would inevitably misuse, document the safety features Al would desperately add, and predict the catastrophic outcome.

MORE POWER Rating: 🚗🚗🚗🚗⬜ 4 out of 5 Hot Rods

The Assignment

Every Binford tool starts with a noble purpose and ends with Tim in the emergency room. Your job is to channel both the engineering optimism of Binford R&D and the chaotic energy of Tim "The Tool Man" Taylor.

Design an original Binford tool that seems like a good idea but has catastrophic potential in Tim's hands. Think: a leaf blower that's "just a little" jet-powered, or a toilet plunger with "revolutionary vacuum suction technology."

Requirements

  • Tool Name & Model Number: Create an official Binford product name with model number (e.g., "Binford 6100 TurboMaster Leaf Annihilator")
  • Marketing Copy: Write 2-3 sentences of enthusiastic Binford marketing speak about your tool's "revolutionary features"
  • Technical Specifications: Include at least 5 specifications (horsepower, voltage, RPM, decibel level, etc.) that sound impressive but are wildly dangerous
  • Warning Labels: Write 5 warning labels that would appear on the tool - the kind that only exist because someone (Tim) already tried it
  • Al's Safety Modifications: Describe 3 safety features Al would add after the first incident, with his resigned commentary
  • Predicted Disaster Scenario: Write a 200-300 word narrative of how Tim would misuse this tool on Tool Time, including Al's reactions and the aftermath

Grading Rubric

Criteria Description Points
Tool Concept Original, plausible (in the Binford universe), and appropriately ridiculous $300
Marketing Copy Captures Binford's overconfident corporate voice $200
Technical Specs Impressive-sounding numbers that clearly spell disaster $200
Warning Labels Specific, absurd, and clearly born from Tim-related incidents $250
Al's Modifications Practical safety improvements with authentic Al frustration $250
Disaster Narrative Vivid, funny, and true to Tool Time format $300
👔

Al's Tips for Success

  • Study actual Binford model numbers from the show - they follow a pattern (usually 4-digit numbers starting with 6 or higher)
  • Real warning labels exist because someone did the thing. Make yours specific: "Do not use near flamingos" suggests a story
  • My safety modifications should be practical but futile - Tim will find a way around them
  • Remember: the humor comes from Tim's confidence, not the destruction itself
  • Include a grunt or two in your disaster scenario. AEUHHH?!
