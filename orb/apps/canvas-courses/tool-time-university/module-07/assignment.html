RANDY'S COLLEGE RECOMMENDATION

Wilson W. Wilson Jr. Writes for His Young Neighbor

Randy Taylor is applying to college, and he's asked his neighbor Wilson to write a recommendation letter. Craft this letter in Wilson's distinctive voice - philosophical, warm, culturally rich, and genuinely supportive of Randy's intellectual journey.

MORE POWER Rating: 🚗⬜⬜⬜⬜ 1 out of 5 Hot Rods (Wilson prefers wisdom to power)

The Assignment

Randy Taylor - the middle child, the witty one, the one who actually reads books and thinks about things - is applying to college. Perhaps it's for environmental science, journalism, or philosophy. Whatever the field, Randy asked Wilson to write his recommendation.

Wilson, of course, said yes. He's watched Randy grow from a sarcastic kid into a thoughtful young man. He's had hundreds of fence conversations with the Taylor boys, but Randy is the one who actually listens and asks follow-up questions about Kierkegaard.

Randy Taylor - Character Profile

  • Middle Child: Between athletic Brad and sensitive Mark
  • Intellectually Curious: Asks questions, challenges assumptions
  • Witty & Sarcastic: Quick comebacks, often at Tim's expense
  • Socially Conscious: Interested in environmental and social issues
  • Growth Arc: From troublemaking middle child to thoughtful young adult
  • Wilson Connection: Most likely Taylor to engage with Wilson's philosophy
  • Real-World Note: In later seasons, Randy went to Costa Rica for an environmental program
Wilson W. Wilson Jr., Ph.D.
7331 W Green Lake Dr N
Detroit, Michigan
(The house behind the tall fence)

To the Admissions Committee,

Hidey-ho! I write to you today regarding one Randall William Taylor, who has been my neighbor for the better part of his formative years. As the Roman philosopher Seneca once wrote, "It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste a lot of it." I can assure you that young Randy has wasted very little of his...

[Your letter continues...]

With warm regards and a tip of my pith helmet,

Wilson W. Wilson Jr., Ph.D.
Cultural Anthropologist, World Traveler,
and Devoted Neighbor

Requirements

  • Proper Letter Format: Return address, date, salutation, closing - formal academic recommendation structure
  • Wilson's Voice: Philosophical quotes, cultural references, warm but thoughtful tone
  • At Least 3 Philosophical/Literary References: Properly attributed, relevant to Randy's qualities
  • Specific Examples: Create 2-3 specific anecdotes of Randy demonstrating intellectual growth or character
  • Randy's Strengths: Academic curiosity, critical thinking, wit, social consciousness
  • Growth Narrative: Show how Randy has developed over time (Wilson has known him for years)
  • Fence Conversation Reference: At least one mention of their conversations over the fence
  • Contrast with Brothers: Subtle acknowledgment that Randy is different from Brad (and that's good)
  • Genuine Warmth: Wilson truly cares about this kid; it should show

Wilson's Writing Voice

Key Wilson Characteristics to Capture:

  • Opening Greeting: Wilson has distinctive greetings ("Hidey-ho," "Well, hello there")
  • Quote Integration: Seamlessly weaves in philosophers, writers, and cultural figures
  • Global Perspective: References cultures from around the world (he's well-traveled)
  • Gentle Wisdom: Never preachy or condescending; shares insight as a gift
  • Self-Deprecating Humor: Occasionally references his own eccentricities
  • Deep Observation: Notices things others miss; truly sees people
  • Academic Credibility: Wilson is actually brilliant - multiple degrees, published work

Al's Tips for Success

  • Wilson quotes philosophers like I cite safety regulations - naturally and frequently
  • He knew Randy when Randy was just a sarcastic kid; show that evolution
  • Wilson is genuinely accomplished - Ph.D., world traveler, published author. His recommendation carries weight.
  • The fence is central to their relationship; it's where wisdom was shared
