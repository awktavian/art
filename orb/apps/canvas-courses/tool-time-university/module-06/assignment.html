AL'S WEDDING TOAST

What Al Borland Would Say on His Big Day

Al Borland is finally getting married. Write his wedding toast - a speech that balances humor and sincerity, references his flannel-filled journey, acknowledges his mother, roasts Tim appropriately, and reveals the heart we always knew was under all that plaid.

MORE POWER Rating: ЁЯЪЧЁЯЪЧтмЬтмЬтмЬ 2 out of 5 Hot Rods (Al prefers moderate power)

The Assignment

After years of dating disasters, mother's interference, and Tim's constant ribbing, Al Borland is getting married. As his best friend (despite everything), you've been asked to write the wedding toast Al himself would give at the reception.

This is Al's moment to shine. The toast should capture his voice - measured, warm, occasionally exasperated - while revealing the genuine emotion underneath. He's not Tim; he won't grunt or make crude jokes. But he's also not boring; he has a dry wit and deep feelings.

*Al stands, adjusts his formal flannel, and clears his throat*

"Thank you all for being here today. For those who don't know me - and after 8 seasons of Tool Time, that seems statistically unlikely - I'm Al Borland."

[polite laughter]

"My mother always said I would find the right person when I stopped looking. She also said I should call more and that my flannel collection was 'becoming a problem.' She was right about two of those things."

[Your toast continues...]

The Flannel Factor

Al's flannel shirts aren't just a wardrobe choice - they're a character trait. The toast should reference flannel at least once, meaningfully. Perhaps his bride accepts his flannel. Perhaps the groomsmen all wore flannel. Perhaps he's finally wearing something else (gasp!).

Requirements

  • Opening: Classic Al introduction - polite, slightly self-deprecating, establishes his voice
  • The Story: How Al met his bride and/or a key moment in their relationship (you create this narrative)
  • The Tim Reference: At least one callback to Tim disasters or "I don't think so, Tim" moments, but affectionate
  • Mother Mention: Al's mother must be acknowledged (she's VERY important in his life)
  • Flannel Reference: Meaningful integration of Al's signature style
  • Sincere Heart: A genuine emotional moment that reveals Al's depth
  • Tool Time Callbacks: At least 2 references to show moments, catchphrases, or running gags
  • The Toast: A proper toast ending that raises glasses and honors the marriage
  • Stage Directions: Include [directions] for pauses, reactions, and moments throughout

Al's Character Notes

Key Al Characteristics to Capture:

  • Measured speech: Al doesn't rush. He pauses. He considers his words.
  • Dry wit: His humor comes from understatement, not exaggeration
  • Competence: He knows what he's doing (unlike Tim) and takes pride in it
  • Patience: Years of Tim have given him legendary patience
  • Hidden depth: Under the flannel is a romantic who believes in doing things right
  • Mother issues: Loving but sometimes smothering relationship with his mom
  • Loyal friend: Despite everything, he genuinely cares about Tim

Al's Tips for Success (Yes, He's Giving Tips About His Own Toast)

  • I speak in complete sentences. Tim doesn't, but I do.
  • When I make a joke, I don't laugh at it myself. The humor comes from delivery.
  • I would absolutely tear up during my own wedding toast. Include that moment.
  • My mother would interrupt if she could. Consider how I'd handle that.
  • Tim as best man would probably do something during my speech. Plan for it.
  • I would have practiced this speech extensively. It would be polished but still feel genuine.
  • A good tool and a good marriage have something in common: quality materials, proper technique, and regular maintenance.
