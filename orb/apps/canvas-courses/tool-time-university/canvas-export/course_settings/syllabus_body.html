Course Description

Does everybody know what time it is? TOOL TIME!

Welcome to an 8-module deep dive into the beloved ABC sitcom Home Improvement (1991-1999), the #1 rated show on American television for multiple seasons. Through lessons, quizzes, discussions, and creative projects, you will explore:

  • Character development and narrative arcs across 204 episodes and 8 seasons
  • The cultural impact of 1990s family sitcoms and the TGIF phenomenon
  • Themes of masculinity, family dynamics, and personal growth
  • Wilson's philosophical wisdom and its real-world sources
  • The "More Power" philosophy and what it reveals about American culture
  • Television production elements and sitcom structure

Hidey-ho, young scholars!

As the great philosopher Aristotle once said, 'We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit.' In this course, you'll develop the habit of critical television analysis - and perhaps discover that the quest for MORE POWER is really a quest for understanding ourselves.

- Wilson W. Wilson Jr., Ph.D.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, successful students will demonstrate mastery in four key areas:

Outcome 1: Media Analysis

Analyze Narrative Structure in Sitcom Format

Identify and analyze the three-act structure, A/B storylines, and comedic timing in sitcom episodes, using Home Improvement as the primary text. Students will recognize how Tool Time segments function as both cold opens and recurring structural elements.

Outcome 2: Character Analysis

Evaluate Character Development Across Series

Trace character arcs across 8 seasons, analyzing how Tim, Jill, the boys, Al, and Wilson grow, change, and maintain consistency within the sitcom format. Connect character evolution to series-long themes.

Outcome 3: Cultural Studies

Contextualize Television Within Historical Period

Analyze how Home Improvement reflects and responds to 1990s American culture, including family values, gender roles, and technological change. Examine the show's complex portrayal of masculinity.

Outcome 4: Creative Writing

Capture Character Voice in Creative Writing

Write in established characters' voices, maintaining consistency with their personalities. Create original dialogue featuring Tim's grunts, Al's "I don't think so, Tim," and Wilson's philosophical framing.

Module Schedule

The course follows the 8-season structure of Home Improvement, with each module focusing on one season. Recommended pace: 1 week per module.

Module 01

Pilot Light: Igniting the Tool Time Flame

Season 1 (1991-1992) - 24 Episodes

Analyze the pilot episode, understand how the show established its core formula, and explore the birth of a TV icon. Learn about the Tool Time chaos, family conflict, and Wilson's wisdom framework.

$6,750 HED
Module 02

Assembly Required: Some Parts Not Included

Season 2 (1992-1993) - 25 Episodes

Explore Heidi's introduction, celebrity guest appearances, and "The Great Race" with Bob Vila. Analyze 90s masculinity, DIY culture, and the tension between enthusiasm and expertise.

$5,250 HED
Module 03

Measure Twice, Cut Once: Al's Philosophy

Season 3 (1993-1994) - 25 Episodes

Deep dive into Al Borland's character. Analyze the Tim/Al dynamic, flannel fashion as character expression, and the value of careful methodical work vs. Tim's enthusiasm.

$5,250 HED
Module 04

Load-Bearing Walls: Structural Integrity

Season 4 (1994-1995) - 26 Episodes

Examine Tim and Jill's marriage communication patterns. Analyze how the show portrays marital conflict resolution and the structural foundation of the Taylor family.

$5,250 HED
Module 05

Rewiring the Sitcom: New Connections

Season 5 (1995-1996) - 26 Episodes

Follow the Taylor boys through adolescence. Create character arc timelines and analyze coming-of-age themes within the sitcom format.

$5,250 HED
Module 06

Heavy Machinery: When Stakes Get Higher

Season 6 (1996-1997) - 25 Episodes

Examine Home Improvement's peak cultural impact. Analyze how the show reflected and shaped 90s American values during its highest-rated seasons.

$5,250 HED
Module 07

The Addition: Building Onto What Works

Season 7 (1997-1998) - 25 Episodes

Track character evolution across the series. Create character development charts and analyze Mark's goth phase as exploration of identity in sitcoms.

$5,250 HED
Module 08

The Final Inspection: Legacy and Farewell

Season 8 (1998-1999) - 28 Episodes

Analyze the series finale and its approach to closure. Explore why Tim Allen and Patricia Richardson turned down $75 million for a 9th season, and examine the show's lasting legacy.

$5,250 HED
Final Project

⭐ Master Craftsman Challenge: Write Your Own Tool Time Episode

Demonstrate everything you've learned by writing a complete 22-minute Tool Time episode script. Include a Tool Time segment, Taylor family storyline, Wilson's philosophical wisdom, and authentic character voices.

$10,000 HED

Course Policies

Late Work Policy

⏰

Tool Time Waits for No One!

Late submissions lose $100 HED per day late, with a maximum deduction of 50%. Like a home renovation that goes over schedule, late work costs you equity! Plan ahead and submit on time.

Academic Integrity

All work must be your own original analysis and writing. Collaboration is encouraged in discussions, but individual assignments must reflect your unique perspective. Plagiarism is as unwelcome as Tim's unsupervised use of a jackhammer.

🤔

Al's Reminder on AI Tools

AI writing assistants may be used for brainstorming and editing, but the core analysis and creative work must be yours. Just as Tim shouldn't rely solely on more power without understanding the fundamentals, you shouldn't rely solely on AI without developing your own analytical skills.

Discussion Participation
