## Course Description

*ARUH ARUH ARUH!*

Welcome to Tool Time University, where we take the beloved ABC sitcom *Home Improvement* (1991-1999) and examine it through the lens of family dynamics, masculinity in the '90s, the philosophy of neighborly wisdom, and the eternal question: "Can you REALLY add a Chevy 350 engine to a household appliance?"

Over eight action-packed modules (one per glorious season), you'll explore the Taylor family's journey from Tim's first "MORE POWER" disaster to the series finale that made us all ugly-cry into our Binford-branded tissue boxes.

This isn't your grandmother's TV studies course. Unless your grandmother is like Al's mom, Alma Borland—in which case, we're sure she'd excel.

**Warning:** This course may cause spontaneous grunting, an unhealthy attachment to flannel, and an irresistible urge to give unsolicited advice over a fence. Side effects may include improved relationships, philosophical enlightenment, and minor burns.

## Final Project: "The Ultimate Tool Time Episode"
**Worth: $10,000 Home Equity Points**

### The Assignment

You will create a comprehensive pitch for a brand-new Tool Time episode that could have aired during any season. This is your chance to prove you've mastered the Taylor family dynamics, Tim's disasters, Al's patience, and Wilson's wisdom.

### Requirements

Your pitch must include:

1. **Episode Title** — In the classic Home Improvement naming style (puns encouraged, nay, REQUIRED)

2. **Cold Open** — A 2-minute Tool Time segment where Tim demonstrates a tool and something goes predictably wrong

3. **A-Plot Synopsis** — The main Taylor family storyline (500 words minimum)

4. **B-Plot Synopsis** — Secondary storyline, usually involving the boys or a supporting character (300 words minimum)

5. **Three Wilson's Fence Scenes** — Complete dialogue for at least three moments where Tim goes to Wilson for advice. Include Wilson's philosophical references and Tim's misinterpretation of them.

6. **Tim's Realization Moment** — The scene where Tim finally gets it (sort of)

7. **Tag Scene** — The final funny moment, usually a callback or one more Tool Time disaster

8. **Cast Notes** — Any guest stars you'd include and why

## Course Policies

### The Tim Taylor Attendance Policy

Much like Tim's commitment to safety protocols, attendance in this course is... flexible but consequential.

- **Present & Grunting (Full Points):** You're there, you're engaged, you might even volunteer for a demonstration
- **Present but Quiet (75% Points):** You're Al-ing it (present, competent, but letting Tim do all the talking)
- **Absent with Notice (50% Points):** You told us you'd be gone (like Tim going to the hardware store and actually coming back with what Jill asked for—rare but appreciated)
- **Absent without Notice (0 Points):** You've pulled a Tim—you're missing and no one knows why, probably involving a trip to the ER

### The Al Borland Academic Integrity Policy

Al would never take credit for Tim's ideas (why would he want to?), and you shouldn't take credit for others' work either.

- All work must be your own original creation
- Citations must be provided for any referenced material
- Plagiarism will result in a "I don't think so, Tim" and a zero on the assignment
- Second offense results in a conversation with Wilson about ethics (and a course failure)

### The Jill Taylor Late Work Policy

Jill manages a household, three boys, Tim's chaos, AND goes back to school. She knows life happens.

- **24 hours late:** 10% penalty (You overslept, it happens)
- **48 hours late:** 25% penalty (You had a Tool Time-level disaster)
- **72+ hours late:** 50% penalty (Al's mom was in town, we understand)
- **After grading period:** No credit (The ship has sailed, like Tim's attempts at sailing)

### The Wilson Accommodation Statement

Just as Wilson accommodates Tim's constant need for advice, we will accommodate students with documented needs.

Please contact the Office of Accessibility Services if you require:
- Extended time on assessments
- Alternative format materials
- A fence to lean on during exams
- Any other reasonable accommodation
