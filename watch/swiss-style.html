Swiss / International Style

1950s — Present

The Swiss International Typographic Style emerged from Basel and Zurich, championing objectivity, mathematical grids, and sans-serif typography. Josef Müller-Brockmann and Max Bill believed design should be clear, legible, and free from personal expression. This watch embodies that rational beauty—Helvetica numerals, precise indices, and the iconic red second hand that became synonymous with Swiss railway clocks.
